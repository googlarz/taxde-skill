# Budgeting Strategies

## 50/30/20 Rule
- 50% Needs: housing, groceries, transport, insurance, healthcare
- 30% Wants: dining, entertainment, subscriptions, travel, clothing
- 20% Savings: emergency fund, investments, extra debt payments

Best for: beginners, simple income structure.

## Zero-Based Budgeting
Every euro gets a job. Income minus all planned expenses = 0.
- Assign every euro to a category before the month starts
- Adjust mid-month only if priorities change

Best for: people who want full control, variable income.

## Envelope Method
Physical or virtual "envelopes" per category. When an envelope is empty, stop spending.
- Set fixed amounts per category
- No borrowing between envelopes

Best for: overspenders in specific categories.

## 80/20 Rule
- Save/invest 20% automatically at the start of the month
- Spend the remaining 80% however you want

Best for: people who hate tracking every purchase.

## Pay Yourself First
- Automate savings and investments before discretionary spending
- Treat savings as a non-negotiable bill

## Berlin-Specific Tips
- Average rent in Berlin: €1,200-1,800 warm for a 2-room flat
- BVG monthly pass: €49 (Deutschlandticket)
- Groceries: budget €250-400/month for a single person
- Rundfunkbeitrag: €18.36/month (mandatory)
- GEZ is per household, not per person
