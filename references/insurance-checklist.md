# Insurance Checklist — Germany / Berlin

## Essential (everyone should have)

### Health Insurance (Krankenversicherung)
- Mandatory in Germany. GKV (public) or PKV (private).
- GKV: ~15.9% of gross (employee pays ~8%); employer covers the rest
- PKV: income must exceed Versicherungspflichtgrenze (€69,300 in 2024)
- Zusatzbeitrag varies by provider (0.9%-2.5%)

### Personal Liability (Privathaftpflicht)
- Covers damage you cause to others. €40-80/year.
- Minimum €10M coverage recommended.
- Essential — a single accident can cost millions.

### Disability Insurance (Berufsunfähigkeitsversicherung)
- Replaces income if you can't work in your profession.
- Get it young — premiums increase with age and health history.
- Cover at least 75% of net income.
- Most important insurance after health.

## Important (situation-dependent)

### Home Contents (Hausratversicherung)
- Covers furniture, electronics, belongings against theft/fire/water.
- €50-150/year depending on apartment size and location.
- Berlin tip: bike theft coverage is worth adding.

### Legal Insurance (Rechtsschutzversicherung)
- Covers legal costs for employment, rental, and traffic disputes.
- €150-300/year. Useful in Germany's lawsuit-heavy legal system.
- Especially valuable for renters (Mietrechtsschutz).

### Life Insurance (Risikolebensversicherung)
- Term life only (not whole life / Kapitallebensversicherung).
- Needed if others depend on your income (partner, children).
- Coverage = 3-5x annual gross income.

## Nice to Have

### Travel Insurance (Reisekrankenversicherung)
- Covers medical costs outside Germany. €10-20/year.
- GKV coverage is limited abroad.

### Dental Supplement (Zahnzusatzversicherung)
- GKV covers basics; supplements cover implants, crowns.
- €15-40/month depending on age and coverage level.
- Get it before you need expensive work done.

## Generally NOT Needed
- Handy/Elektronik insurance (overpriced for the risk)
- Sterbegeldversicherung (funeral insurance — save the money instead)
- Kapitallebensversicherung (whole life — bad investment vehicle)
- Glass insurance (Glasversicherung — low risk, cheap to replace)
