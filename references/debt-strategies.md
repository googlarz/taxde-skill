# Debt Payoff Strategies

## Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Put all extra money toward the HIGHEST interest rate debt
3. When it's paid off, redirect to the next highest rate

Saves the most money in interest.

## Snowball Method (Psychologically Optimal)
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Put all extra money toward the SMALLEST balance debt
3. When it's paid off, redirect to the next smallest

Builds momentum through quick wins.

## When to Use Which
- Avalanche: large interest rate differences between debts
- Snowball: many small debts causing decision fatigue, need motivation
- Hybrid: pay off one small debt for momentum, then switch to avalanche

## Mortgage Optimization
- Extra payments: even €100/month extra can save years and thousands in interest
- Sondertilgung: most German mortgages allow 5-10% extra payment per year
- Refinancing: compare when your Zinsbindung ends; shop around 6 months early
- KfW loans: check if you qualify for subsidized rates (energy efficiency, etc.)

## Debt-to-Income Ratio
- Healthy: below 36% of gross income
- Concerning: 36-50%
- Critical: above 50% — consider debt counseling (Schuldnerberatung)

## German-Specific Notes
- Schufa: late payments reported after 90 days, stay on record 3 years
- Dispo (overdraft): typically 8-14% interest — avoid carrying a balance
- Rahmenkredit: often cheaper than Dispo if you need credit flexibility
- Privatinsolvenz: last resort, 3-year process to discharge debt
