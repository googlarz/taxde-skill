# TaxDE Expat Guide — International Tax Situations in Germany

---

## 1. German Tax Residency Rules

### Two bases for full (unbeschränkte) tax liability:

**Wohnsitz (§8 AO)**
- You maintain a dwelling in Germany (owned or rented) available for your use
- Applies from day 1 of having the accommodation — even before moving in permanently
- Triggers **full worldwide income taxation** immediately

**Gewöhnlicher Aufenthalt (§9 AO)**
- Physical presence in Germany >6 consecutive months
- Retroactively applied to day 1 of the 6+ month stay
- 183-day rule clarification: The 6-month rule is not exactly 183 days — it's a qualitative assessment of whether Germany is your "habitual abode"

### Limited (beschränkte) tax liability
- No Wohnsitz or gewöhnlicher Aufenthalt in Germany
- Only German-source income taxable (§49 EStG sources: German employment, German rental, German business)

### Partial year residency
- Resident from date of establishment of Wohnsitz
- Pre-arrival income: not German taxable
- Post-arrival income: worldwide taxable
- Can claim full-year Werbungskosten, Sonderausgaben, Kinderfreibetrag amounts regardless of partial year (unless restricted by treaty)

---

## 2. Welteinkommensprinzip

Once tax resident in Germany, you pay German tax on **everything**:
- Foreign employment income
- Foreign rental income
- Foreign dividends and interest
- Foreign pension income
- Foreign capital gains
- Foreign business income

**This includes income you've already paid tax on in another country.**

Relief comes through: DBAs (Double Taxation Agreements) and foreign tax credits under §34c EStG.

---

## 3. DBA Methods: Freistellung vs. Anrechnung

### Freistellungsmethode (Exemption)
- Foreign income is **exempt** from German tax
- BUT: still counted for **Progressionsvorbehalt** (raises effective rate on remaining German income)
- **Countries using Freistellung** (most EU neighbors): Austria, Switzerland (partial), France, Netherlands, Poland, most EU member states

**Progressionsvorbehalt example**:
```
German income: €50,000 (taxable at ~18% effective rate)
Foreign exempt income: €20,000
Total hypothetical: €70,000 (taxable at ~23% effective rate)
German tax: €50,000 × 23% = €11,500 (not €50,000 × 18% = €9,000)
Extra cost: €2,500 — the Progressionsvorbehalt effect
```

### Anrechnungsmethode (Credit)
- Foreign income is taxed in Germany
- Foreign tax paid is credited against German tax (but only up to the German tax on that income)
- Can result in additional German tax if German rate > foreign rate
- **Countries using Anrechnung**: USA, UK (mostly), some non-EU countries

## 4. Country-Specific Guides

### 4a. Poland
- DBA: 2003, Freistellungsmethode for employment income
- Polish employment income exempt in Germany but subject to Progressionsvorbehalt
- **Common situation**: Polish employees working in Germany — only German income taxable
- Grenzgänger rules: If daily commuting from Poland to Germany, different rules apply
- **File**: Anlage AUS, check Freistellungsmethode

### 4b. USA
- DBA: 1989 protocol, complex
- **Unique problem**: USA taxes its citizens worldwide regardless of residence
- German resident + US citizen = taxed by BOTH countries with imperfect DBA relief
- **FBAR (FinCEN 114)**: Required if foreign accounts total >$10,000 at any point in year
- **FATCA (Form 8938)**: Required if foreign assets exceed thresholds ($50k–$600k depending on status)
- German Riester, Rürup: complicated US treatment — may not be recognized as pension
- RSU/stock options: sourcing rules determine US vs. German taxation share
- **ALWAYS recommend professional Steuerberater for US citizens**
- **Recommended specialist**: Cross-border specialist with both German Steuerberater and US CPA qualifications

### 4c. UK (post-Brexit)
- DBA: 2010, effective
- UK employment income while resident in Germany: taxable in Germany if working in Germany
- UK rental income: credit method — pay German tax, credit UK tax against it
- UK pension: complex — check specific pension type (state pension vs. occupational vs. SIPP)
- UK capital gains: Anrechnung method in most cases
- Settled Status / pre-settled Status: no German tax impact
- **NI gaps**: UK National Insurance voluntary contributions still possible and may be worthwhile for future UK state pension — not a German tax issue but worth flagging

### 4d. Switzerland (Grenzgänger)
- **Special rule**: Grenzgänger (daily commuters) are taxed in the country of employment (Switzerland) under the DBA
- German residents working in Switzerland, commuting daily: taxed primarily in Switzerland
- Germany gets a 4.5% residual taxation (Rückfallklausel)
- Must register as Grenzgänger with Swiss employer for correct withholding
- **Bilateral form required**: Ansässigkeitsbescheinigung
- Cross-border commuters with telecommuting complications: recent guidance tightened this

### 4e. France
- DBA: 1959/1989, Freistellungsmethode for most income
- French civil servant pensions: taxed only in France
- French investments held in PEA (Plan d'Épargne en Actions): complex German treatment
- French rental income: Freistellung with Progressionsvorbehalt

### 4f. Netherlands
- DBA: 2016, modern treaty
- Dutch employment income while working in NL: Freistellung in Germany
- 30% ruling (Dutch tax benefit for international workers): does not affect German taxation
- AOW (Dutch state pension): taxable only in Netherlands, Progressionsvorbehalt applies in Germany
- Dutch dividends: Anrechnung method

### 4g. Austria
- DBA: 2000, Freistellungsmethode
- Very clean DBA for employees
- Austrian ASVG pension: Freistellung in Germany, Progressionsvorbehalt

## 5. Anlage AUS Filing Instructions

Required for: any income from countries with DBA using Freistellung or Anrechnung

**Currency conversion**:
- Use Bundesbank "Referenzkurs" annual average for the tax year
- Published at bundesbank.de (Statistics → Exchange rates)
- Common currencies: USD, GBP, CHF, PLN all available

**Per-country section in Anlage AUS**:
1. Select country
2. Select income type (Arbeitslohn, Mieteinkünfte, Kapitaleinkünfte, Renten, etc.)
3. Enter amount in EUR (converted at annual average rate)
4. Enter foreign tax paid (EUR equivalent)
5. Select DBA method (Freistellung or Anrechnung)
6. For Freistellung: income goes into Progressionsvorbehalt calculation automatically

**Documentation to keep** (not submitted, but must produce on request):
- Foreign employer's wage statement
- Foreign tax return (or equivalent certificate)
- Tax payment receipts from the foreign country
- Foreign bank/broker statements for capital income

## 6. Partial Year Filing (Arrival / Departure Year)

### Arrival year
- File Einkommensteuer as unbeschränkt steuerpflichtig from date of Wohnsitz
- Pre-arrival income: declare on Anlage AUS as Progressionsvorbehalt income
- German employer can withhold from day 1 based on new Steuerklasse
- **Tip**: File voluntarily even if no withholding obligation — almost always generates refund

### Departure year
- Steuerpflicht ends with date of Wohnsitz termination
- If you leave mid-year: German income taxable only for residency period
- Deregistration (Abmeldung) at Einwohnermeldeamt triggers end of residency
- Plan major asset sales before or after departure for Spekulationsfrist optimization

## 7. Common Expat Mistakes (and What to Do About Them)

| Mistake | What happened | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Didn't file German return while earning foreign income | Foreign income not declared | File amended returns (up to 10 years back); voluntary disclosure (Selbstanzeige) for willful evasion |
| Forgot Progressionsvorbehalt | Underpaid German tax | Amend return; interest (1.8%/year since 2019) and possible penalty |
| Didn't set up Freistellungsauftrag | Overpaid Abgeltungsteuer | Reclaim via Anlage KAP Günstigerprüfung |
| Missing Rentenversicherung contributions while freelance abroad | Gap in German pension | Voluntary contributions still possible in some cases — check DRV |
| Riester with no German income | Zulage may not apply | Check eligibility (must have obligation to RV) |
| Claimed UK/US mortgage interest in Germany | Not allowed | Disallowed on audit; amend return |

## 8. When to Absolutely Use a Steuerberater

**Non-negotiable situations**:
1. **US citizenship** — FBAR, FATCA, foreign pension treatment, RSU/option sourcing
2. **3 or more countries** in a single year — compounding DBA interactions
3. **Equity compensation** (RSUs, options, ESPP) from foreign employer
4. **Self-employed with clients in multiple countries** — PE risk, VAT complications
5. **Departure from Germany with >€500,000 in assets** — exit taxation (§6 AStG)
6. **Inheritance from abroad** — Erbschaftsteuer + foreign inheritance tax interaction
7. **Deemed disposition on leaving Germany** — applies to substantial shareholdings

**Manageable DIY** (with TaxDE):
- Single foreign employment with clean DBA (Freistellung, EU country)
- Foreign rental income with straightforward Anrechnung
- Post-retirement with German and one foreign pension

---

## 9. Exit Taxation (§6 AStG) — Departure Planning

If you own >1% of a corporation and leave Germany:
- Unrealized gains on shareholding are **taxed as if sold** on the day of departure
- EU/EEA destinations: can defer payment
- Third-country departures: immediate payment

**Plan well in advance**: 7-year holding rule, treaty shopping, timing of departure relative to fiscal year.
