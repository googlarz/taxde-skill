# Investment Basics

## Core Principles
1. Start early — compound interest is your biggest advantage
2. Diversify — don't put all eggs in one basket
3. Keep costs low — index funds beat most active managers
4. Stay invested — time in market beats timing the market
5. Rebalance periodically — maintain your target allocation

## Asset Classes
- **Stocks/ETFs**: Higher return, higher volatility. Core of growth portfolio.
- **Bonds**: Lower return, lower volatility. Stability anchor.
- **Real Estate**: Illiquid, but inflation hedge and rental income.
- **Cash**: Zero real return after inflation, but fully liquid.
- **Commodities**: Inflation hedge, high volatility.

## Common Allocations by Risk Tolerance
- Conservative: 30% stocks, 50% bonds, 20% cash
- Moderate: 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% cash
- Aggressive: 80% stocks, 15% bonds, 5% cash

## Tax-Advantaged Investing in Germany
- Sparer-Pauschbetrag: €1,000 single / €2,000 married (2024+)
- Freistellungsauftrag: set up at your broker to avoid withholding on first €1,000
- Riester: state-subsidized pension (€175/year + child bonuses)
- Rürup/Basisrente: tax-deductible pension for self-employed
- bAV: employer-sponsored pension with tax/social contribution advantages

## FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)
- FIRE Number = Annual Expenses / Withdrawal Rate
- Standard withdrawal rate: 4% (Trinity Study)
- Conservative: 3.5% withdrawal rate
- Example: €30,000/year expenses → €750,000 FIRE number at 4%
